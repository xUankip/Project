Tokbokki

| Describe

Steaming hot Tokbokki with chewy rice cakes coated in a spicy chili sauce, accompanied by fish cakes and green onions, is the perfect snack for chilly days. Tokbokki boasts the characteristic heat of Korean chili, the mild sweetness of rice cakes, and the harmonious saltiness of soy sauce, resulting in an irresistible flavor. Tokbokki is a ubiquitous street food in South Korea, cherished for its delicious taste and affordable price.

| How-to guide on how to cook Tokbokki

1. Ingredients

  • 500g Tteokbokki rice cakes
  • 200g Korean fish cake
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 100g cabbage
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp Korean chili paste
  • 2 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 500ml water
  • Cooking oil

2. Cook

  1. Prepare ingredient
    • Rinse the Tteokbokki rice cakes, soak in cold water for about 30 minutes to soften.
    • Cut the Korean fish cake into bite-sized pieces.
    • Peel the onion, rinse it clean, and cut it into wedges
    • Peel the carrot, rinse it clean, and cut it into long strips.
    • Rinse the cabbage clean, and cut it into bite-sized pieces.
    • Mince the garlic
  2. Cook Tokbokki
    • Heat cooking oil in a pan, stir-fry the minced garlic until fragrant.
    • Add the Korean fish cake and stir-fry until slightly crispy.
    • Add the onion, carrot, and cabbage and stir-fry together.
    • Add Korean chili paste, tomato ketchup, sugar, salt, and pepper and stir-fry well.
    • Add water to the pot and bring to a boil.
    • Add the Tteokbokki rice cakes to the pot and cook for about 15 minutes until the rice cakes are soft.
    • Season to taste.
